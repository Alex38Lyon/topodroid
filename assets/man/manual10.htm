[10] SURVEY INFO WINDOW

Before we get to take measurements let's look at the survey informations dialog. You open it by long tapping on the survey entry in the main window, or with the appropriate menu of the shot window.

BUTTONS

  • Notes: to edit the survey annotations. Use the annotation to write down anything that can be useful later. You can edit the global survey annotations also from the shot window and from the sketch window
  • Stats displays some statistics: the number of (regular) legs and their total length, the number of duplicate legs, and that of surface leg. The number of splay shots, that of stations, the number of loops and the number of disconnected surveys (this is 1 if the legs are all attached together in one survey)
  • 3D: the 3D display. This is the same as the 3D menu of the shot-list activity
  • GPS [A]: to enter (or get with the GPS) the coordinates of a fixed station, eg, the entrance. The fixed points coordinates that have already been defined, are listed.
  • Photos [A] lists the pictures of the survey. Tapping one of them you get a dialog to edit the description or delete the photo
  • Sensors [A]: shows the list of sensor measurements of the survey. At the moment these are not editable.

MENUS

  • Export exports the survey data in the format for your favourite PC program. You have the following choices: Compass, cSurvey, Survex, PocketTopo, Therion, VisualTopo, and Walls. Furthermore the data can be exported as DXF, and CSV text file. If the survey has a fixed station it can be exported as KML, and track file (PLT). This export fails if no the survey station is fixed. Finally you can export a ZIP archive with all the data of the survey.
  • Delete deletes the survey and all associated data (photos, notes, sketch files, etc. ) except the zip archive. There is a confirmation dialog. Make sure to export a ZIP archive before you do it as this is non-recoverable action.
  • Calibration lets the user specify the instruments calibration values for manually entered shots. These values are subtracted to the measured values entered in the shot data dialog, and are taken with the same units as the shot data. For example a calibration length of 1.0 means that the tape is 1.0 m short, ie, measurements are taken from the 1 m mark. The calibration is cleared whenever a survey is opened.
  • Settings (same as the shot window settings)
  • Help

Now you can go and take measurements in your cave, download the data to TopoDroid, assign the stations, and so on. After a few shots you probably need to do some sketching of the cave. Here comes the Sketch button. It displays the list of sketches for the survey. Unless you are very lucky and the cave is simple and straightforward you will need more than one sketch for the survey.
