[12] SKETCH DRAWING

The canvas shows the midline with the splay shots. You can move it around dragging it with one (or two) finger(s), zoom in/out with two fingers. It is not possible to rotate the canvas; the plan has the North upward, and the profile is oriented with the vertical. Tap the Draw button: the app goes in draw mode and the button is highlighted with a white border. Now you can draw with your finger (two fingers still move and zoom). With the undo and redo button you cancel the last drawn or deleted objects and put them back again. Of course you can go back with undo until the canvas is empty.

By default you are drawing "wall" (red) lines. The sketch would be very boring if you had only one line style to use. The tool picker button allows you to pick another line style, or an "area" style (a closed region), or iconic symbols. Tap the button, select the tab (POINT, LINE or AREA) and double-tap the drawing tool you need. You can also select the tool and press the Android "Back" button to return to the canvas. The current drawing tool will be the last you chose before closing the dialog.
You can adjust the line thickness in the Settings dialog. There are separate options for the centerline/splays and the drawing line thickness.

You can also correct mistakes in the drawing. The Undo button lets you remove the last item. What if you need to erase a line or a symbol that you drew long ago, but do not want to loose all the intermediate work? You have two choices: the Erase and the Edit button. The former is very intuitive: it cancels everything you pass your finger over. It is just like a real eraser on the paper: if you pass it in the middle of a line it erases only the central portion (thus leaving two lines).

The Edit [A] button is not so intuitive, but it is very powerful, as it allows pointwise editing operations. In this mode all the drawn items are shown as little green dots. You have to select the item to edit by tapping on it. If there are several items close together, they will all be selected (and shown with a pink circle), but only one will be chosen for editing (big green circle). It may not be what you wanted, but never mind: move among the selected point with the Prev-Next button.
You can adjust the size of the green dots and the selection radius in the Settings dialog.

Segments of Bezier lines have two control points. These are stored with the segment end-point and when this is the selected editing point the two control points are shown as pink circles. Each of them can be moved around by dragging it with the finger.

To clear the selection tap somewhere else in the canvas. To move the editing point, drag it with the finger. To change the properties of the sketch item tap the note button. You can delete (remove) the item or change its Therion properties:

  • point symbols: the "scale", the "orientation", the "text" (for labels), and generic "options"
  • lines: "reverse", "outline", and "options". Lines can also be made more "rocky" (with fewer points).
  • areas: border "visibility"

Editing applies also to shots and stations.
  • shots: stations, "extend", flags, and auto-walls [E].
  • stations: can be made the "current" station, toggle the midline "hiding" attribute [A], and add as Therion "station point" to the scrap (if the autostation option of TopoDroid is not enabled)

The "current" station is where the next downloaded shots will be attached. When a leg is downloaded (or inserted) the new station becomes the "current" station. By changing the "current" station you can continue surveying from a station in the middle of the survey.

Midline hiding

Hiding stations [A] limits the display of the midline for this sketch. This is useful if when the survey is complicated or goes over itself. In this case you might want to see only a part of the midline. Select a station and tap the note button. Then you have the option to hide the previous or next stations. Repeat to show them again. Stations that limit the midline are colored in blue (when the previous are hidden) or red (when the next are hidden).

Point-wise edit

The second to the right button applies only to drawing items and has a drop-down menu of advanced editing actions.
  • Snap to nearby point: move the point to coincide with the closest nearby point (no action if there is no nearby point) [points, lines, areas]
  • Snap to nearby line move to point to coincide with the closest line point, and replace the pieces of the area border before and after it, with pieces that follow the line path [areas]
  • Split the point in two, actually duplicating it and inserting a point in the line/area [lines, areas]
  • Cut the line at the point, thus making two lines [lines]
  • Straighten the line segment removes the control points [lines, areas]
  • Smooth the line segment inserts control points. These are put at 1/3 and 2/3 along the segment. Further editing can move them and give any wanted curvature to the segment. [lines, areas]
  • Remove the line/area point [lines, areas]

Auto-walls [E]

With auto-walls you tell TopoDroid to draw walls to the right and left of a shots interpolating through the end-points of the splays. At the moment there is only one mechanism, namely "convex" walls. A polyline is traced connecting the outermost end-points of the splays. To create a wall there must be at least one splay end-point on the side of the shot.

Through the settings you can specify a limit to the inclination of the splays to take into account, and the amount of allowed concavity.

Do not forget to export the drawings in cSurvey format. This is a special export, because it includes both plan and profile in a single file, and it adds also the survey data. If your survey is simple and has a single sketch you can easily get a final map from this file.
